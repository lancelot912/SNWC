THE SOUTHERN NEVADA WATER CRISIS PROJECT

is a community centered project made up of residents and volunteers through Southern Nevada to help give awawreness of what's going on at Lake Mead. As our population grows, the need for clean water is essential for us to thrive in our community, but also taking care for a better future. This website is not only informational, but also interactive where you can report water waste, know more about the lake and it's history, and most importantly, help Lake Mead in different ways to conserve water. Water levels in Southern Nevada is now in historic record lows and we have to do something about it before it is too late.

Here are some quick information about Lake Mead.

Origin

Lake Mead is named after Elwood Mead, the commissioner of the U.S. Bureau of Reclamation from 1924 to 1936, during the planning and construction of the Boulder Canyon Project that created the Hoover Dam and lake.

#1 Reservoir

International Provider

The lake serves water to the states of Arizona, California, and Nevada, as well as some of Mexico, providing sustenance to nearly 20 million people and large areas of farmland.

Plane Down Under

At the bottom of the lake is a Boeing B-29 Superfortress that crashed in 1948 while testing a prototype missile guidance system, together with at least two smaller airplanes.
